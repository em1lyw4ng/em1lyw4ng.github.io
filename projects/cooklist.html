Context

May 2024 – August 2024 / Product Management Intern / Python, Figma, Excel

Cooklist is an all-in-one application streamlining the way people meal plan, grocery shop, and cook. Their mission is to help people save money, reduce waste, and eat better by showing users what they can cook with what ingredients they have.

As soon as I graduated in the spring of 2024, I specifically reached out to and was subsequently hired as an intern by Cooklist to imagine what an improved pantry management experience looks like. I'm personally a user of the app.

Breaking Down the Problem

From our data analysis, we found that users interact with the pantry in various ways and fluctuate greatly between being proactive vs. reactive when it comes to adding items to and removing from it. We identified common management behaviors among 2,000 users and synthesized them into 3 personas:

Ubituitously across all personas, users express "this app takes some time and devotion in order to work the way it’s intended" because burden is placed on them to cross-check their digital pantry with items in their real-life home pantry. This includes sifting through items to verify that it's expired or relying on memory of what was used before deleting from the app.


Within context, approximately 56% of our users are women ages 18-34, busy people who commonly demonstrated that adopting a new habit, especially one that requires effort, can be a big commitment for someone with a busy schedule. Thus, users who don't have the capacity for pantry upkeep tend to feel overwhelmed and begin to view the app more as a task than a helpful kitchen assistant upon seeing the number of outdated or unverified items accumulate in the app.

Pantry System Update

Our users showed these key insights that helped guide our solution:

  1. Users who download productivity apps expect a level of ease and high return on utility
  2. It’s difficult for users to invest time in adopting a digital experience
  3. Users are busy and have a low tolerance for manual tasks, they seek a balance between maintaining autonomy and benefiting from technology

This led us to redesign the pantry update system and improved our algorithm to identify likely expired or used items, additionally giving users the option to automatically remove them. This redesign emphasizes transparency and user control while reducing manual effort, ultimately helping users keep the pantry up-to-date with less effort.
